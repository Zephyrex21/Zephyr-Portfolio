Put your .vrm file here, named alya.vrm (or update the url prop on
<VrmAvatar /> in App.jsx to match whatever filename you use).

Once you've made your custom model in VRoid Studio, export it as .vrm and
drop it in this folder.

For a quick test before your VRoid model is ready, you can grab a free
sample VRM from the three-vrm project itself (the library this app uses):

  https://github.com/pixiv/three-vrm/blob/dev/packages/three-vrm/examples/models/VRM1_Constraint_Twist_Sample.vrm

Download it, rename it to alya.vrm, and put it here. It won't look like
Alya, but it'll prove the rendering/lip-sync/expression pipeline works
before you've finished the real model.
